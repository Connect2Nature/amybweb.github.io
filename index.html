About Me

My journey starts in the Green and White River Valley, up the foothills to the Muckleshoot Prairie. In my youth I imagined myself advocating for troubled youth. However, this wasnt the course I was destined to take. As a young adult, I ended up working with the Muckleshoot Tribe and navigating landowners through the buracratic web of federal Indian land policy.

Then, onward I went up the White River, to the forested Cascade Mountains along the Greenwater River in 2016. My senses were hightened while exploring the contours of the land and life it supports from plants, to wildlife and people. I was honored to work with Muckleshoot Youth and learn about traditional foods and medicines and the rich coast salish culture from the elders. My heart felt at home in the forest and among the Muckleshoot people, but life took another course. Fall of 2016, I returned to Tacoma and decided to emerse myself in the urban landscape. I dove into my mission to connect people to nature, and was determined to bring the nature I found in the forest, to the city. As well, to re-connect people back to their food systems. I become the coordinator for UWT's community garden and grew food and community.

Learnings I have gained along my travels have been many. I have found that everything is interconnected, thus there is a great need to ensure that we are evaluating throughly, how our decisions will impact not only the economy, but more importanlty the environment, and our communities small and large.

My passion to connect people to nature, preserve traditional knowledges and change the narrative of the history that has shaped our spaces; has drawn me to the Universtiy of Washington's Masters of Science in Geospatial and Technologies and Metro Parks Tacoma. This next adventrue I am on will be one that includes data, geography, technologies, history, culture, plants, animals, and renewal.

I am a forever student and I love exploring the beautiful Pacific Northwest!
